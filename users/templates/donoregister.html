Frequently Asked Questions

Most healthy adults aged 18-65 can donate. You must weigh at least 50kg and meet certain health criteria. There are some restrictions based on medical history, recent travel, and other factors.

Whole blood donors can donate every 56 days. Plasma donors can donate more frequently, typically every 28 days. Platelet donors can donate up to 24 times per year.

Yes, donating blood is very safe. We use sterile, single-use equipment for each donor. The process is supervised by trained medical professionals. Most donors feel fine after donating, though some may experience minor dizziness.

The actual blood donation takes about 8-10 minutes. However, you should plan for about an hour total, which includes registration, a health screening, the donation itself, and a short recovery period with refreshments.
